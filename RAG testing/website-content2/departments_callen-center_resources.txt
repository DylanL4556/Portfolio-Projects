Peer observation of teaching is a collaborative process in which faculty members observe each other’s classroom or online instruction with the goal of improving teaching effectiveness. Rather than serving as an evaluative tool, such observations are typically formative in nature, fostering professional development through constructive feedback and reflective dialogue. This process allows instructors to share best practices, gain new instructional strategies, and deepen their understanding of student engagement and learning.

Disability Policy
If you have a physical, psychological, and/or learning disability that might affect your performance in this class, please contact the Office of Disability Services located in the Educational Support Building Suite 104, phone number is 803-643-6815. Title IX
Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972 is a federal law that prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex of students and employees of educational institutions that receive federal financial assistance. USC Aiken does not discriminate on the basis of sex in its educational programs and in other activities operated by the University and is required by Title IX, and specifically 34 C.F.R. § 106.11, not to discriminate in such a manner. This extends to current students, current employees, and applicants for employment or admission to the University.
If you or someone you know has experienced sex discrimination, sexual violence and/or harassment by any member of the University community, you are encouraged to report the conduct to USC Aiken’s Coordinator of Title IX & Civil Rights at (803) 641-3442. Additional resources are available here.
Mental Health
Mental health concerns or stressful events may lead to diminished academic performance or reduce a student's ability to participate in daily activities. Confidential and free services are available to assist you with addressing these and other concerns you may be experiencing. You can learn more about mental health services available on campus and in the community via the Counseling Center website or by calling 803-641-3609. If you are having a mental health emergency, you may call the Aiken County HELP LINE at 877-648-9900.

Academic Support Services
Academic support services are available in the Student Success Center in the Library. These services include academic coaching, tutoring, the Math Lab, and advising assistance.
The "Thank a Colleague" initiative is a campus-wide effort that encourages faculty and staff to recognize and celebrate the contributions of their colleagues. Whether it's for providing support during a busy semester, leading a successful project, or simply being a dependable team member, this initiative offers a way to acknowledge the everyday efforts that often go unnoticed. Participants can submit brief messages of appreciation that are shared with the recipient, fostering a culture of gratitude, collaboration, and mutual respect.
Peer mentoring is a supportive relationship in which campus colleagues provide guidance, encouragement, and share knowledge to make the transition to USCA smooth and productive. Peers are identified by their respective Deans or Chairs and are paired with an incoming faculty member often in their same department or division. The benefits of peer mentoring are mutual: mentors reinforce their own understanding and leadership skills, while mentees gain confidence, insight, and a sense of belonging. All identified faculty receive peer mentor training offered through the Callen Center for Engaged Teaching and Learning.
