Requirements
Here’s what you need to be admitted as a Freshman undergraduate at USCA:
- Complete the 21 high school course prerequisites at the college prep or higher level (honors, advanced placement, dual credit, etc.)
- This includes 4 units of English, 4 units of Mathematics (Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, or Higher Level), 4 units of academic electives, 3 units of Laboratory Science (Biology, Chemistry, or Higher Level), 3 units of Social Science, 2 units of Foreign Language (must be sequential), and 1 unit of Physical Education or ROTC.
- Demonstrate strong high school performance (through curriculum attempted, grades earned, and class rank)
- Exceed minimum SAT or ACT standards (excluding writing test results)
- Score appropriately on the Admissions Index
*The Admissions Index is calculated using your high school grade point average of core college prep courses and your SAT/ACT scores. Of these two variables, your high school grade point average is the most important and has twice as much weight in the formula.
Note: The University may offer provisional admission for high school students who do not meet the requirements for regular freshman admissions but demonstrate academic potential.
All freshman applicants must submit the following items:
- Freshman application with a $45 application fee
- Official high school transcript

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
USCA accepts applications on a rolling basis throughout the year. It’s recommended that students in their final year of high school complete the application for admission during the fall to allow plenty of time to process it. Once we have all pieces of your application of admissions (application, transcript, and fee), we will work diligently to determine your admissions status.
Ensure you have submitted all material for admission (application fee, high school transcript). You can call the Office of Admissions at (803) 641-3366 to check your status.
Make sure to apply and be admitted before February 1 of your senior year to qualify for our merit-based scholarships potentially. Scholarships are awarded based on a combination of test scores (optional but preferred for scholarship consideration), GPA, and class rank.
