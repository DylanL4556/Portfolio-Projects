The Leadership USCA certificate enhances studentsí ability to transition into career and community roles after college.
Participants will take four required classes
- ASUP 110 ñ Emerging Leaders (2 credit hours)
- ASUP 210 ñ Citizen Leadership (2 credit hours)
- ASUP 215 ñ Diversity and Leadership (2 credit hours)
- ASUP 410 ñ College to Career (1 credit hours)
Mentor
- Toured mentorís work setting
- Meeting #1
- Meeting #2
- Mentee evaluations/Reflection paper
Participate in at least one immersive leadership program
- Alternative Breaks
- Study Abroad Program (either sponsored through an academic department on campus or the International Programs Office)
- Leadership Exchanges (such as USCA/Suffolk Leadership Exchange, Disney Leadership Exchange, or any other of the ASUP 300 level courses)
- Multi-day conference affiliated with the USCA or another national organization.
Participate in at least one experiential/career preparation program
- BADM 429 ñ Internship (1-3 credit hours)
- EDEL 470 ñ Internship in the Elementary School (12 credit hours)
- EDEX 472 ñ Internship in Special Education (12 credit hours)
- EDSE 471 ñ Internship in the Middle or High School (12 credit hours)
- NURS 421/423 ñ Leadership and Management in Nursing for RNs (5 credit hours)
- ARTS 549 ñ Directed Internship (3 credit hours)
- MUED 470 ñ Internship in Music Education (12 credit hours)
- THEA 596 ñ Directed Internship in Theatre (3 credit hours)
- ENGL 497 ñ Directed Internship (1 ñ 3 credit hours)
- EXSC 439 ñ Internship in Fitness Management 3-12 credit hours)
- EXSC 441 ñ Internship in Allied Health (3 credit hours)
- COMM 497 ñ Directed Capstone Internship (3 credit hours)
- COMM 499 ñ Service Learning Capstone Experience (3 credit hours)
- Any other academic credit internship or practicum class
- Non-Credit Internship/Co-Operative Education position
- Job Shadowing Experience
Participants will complete the following options
- Three leadership development opportunities (examples are student organization officers, retreat, orientation leader, etc.)
- Three career development opportunities (job, workshop, etc.)
- Participate in one community volunteer program or event (sponsored by USCA)
- Participate in one health/wellness program or event (sponsored by USCA)
- Participate in one diversity program or event (sponsored by USCA)
Post-Certificate Survey
- Survey completed
Meetings
- Meet every semester with the Leadership Certificate Staff Coordinator
For more information, please email Corey Feraldi, Director of Career Services, at coreyf@usca.edu or Ahmed Samaha, Vice Chancellor of Student Engagement and Belonging, at ahmeds@usca.edu.
