Appendix 1: Examples of Prohibited Conduct
Discrimination:
Examples of potential discrimination under this Policy include but are not limited to those that result in the interference with, limitation, or denial of access to:
- An employee𠏋, or applicant for employment𠏋, access to employment or conditions and benefits of employment (e.g., hiring, advancement, assignment).
- A student𠏋, or admission applicant𠏋, ability to participate in, access, or benefit from educational programs, services, or activities (e.g., admission, academic standing, grades, assignment, campus housing; athletics).
- An authorized volunteer𠏋 ability to participate in a volunteer activity.
- A guest𠏋 or visitor𠏋 ability to participate in, access, or benefit from the university𠏋 programs or activities.
- Failing to provide reasonable accommodations, consistent with state and federal law, to a qualified individual with a disability.
- Failing to accommodate religious beliefs of students, faculty, and staff.
Hostile Environment:
Examples of hostile environments may include but are not limited to:
- Threatening or causing physical harm, extreme verbal abuse, or other conduct which threatens or endangers the health or safety of any person;
- Intimidation, defined as implied threats or acts that cause an unreasonable fear of harm in another;
- Hazing as defined further at ASTAF 115 - Hazing Policy (usca.edu).
- Bullying, defined as repeated and/or severe aggressive behavior likely to intimidate hurt, control, or diminish another person, physically or mentally, that is not speech or conduct otherwise protected by the First Amendment.
Sexual Exploitation:
Examples of sexual exploitation include, but are not limited to:
- observing another individual𠏋 nudity or sexual activity, or allowing another to observe the same, without the knowledge and consent of all parties involved;
- exposing one𠏋 genitals in non-consensual circumstances, including unwelcome sexting;
- streaming of images, photography, video, or audio recording of sexual activity or nudity, or distribution of such without the knowledge and consent of all parties involved;
- prostituting another individual;
- knowingly exposing another individual to a sexually-transmitted disease (STD) or infection (STI) or human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) without the individual𠏋 knowledge;
- knowingly failing to use contraception, or deliberately removing or compromising contraception (Stealthing) without the other party𠏋 knowledge; and/or
- inducing incapacitation (e.g. through the distribution of alcohol or other drug, such as
