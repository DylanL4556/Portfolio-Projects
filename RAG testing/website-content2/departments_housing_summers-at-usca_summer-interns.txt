Summer Interns
Thank you for taking an interest in utilizing University Housing over the summer semester. We are so excited to have you! Please read the information below that pertains to the dates that interns can use our facilities, the features we have available, the process of how to pay, etc. If you have any questions, please call Datasia Padgett at 803-641-3566 or email at datasia.padgett@usca.edu.
The Summer Intern Application is due Friday, May 1st, 2026.
Housing accommodations are based on a first-come, first-served basis.
Check-in and Check-out Information
Summer Intern Housing is available from Sunday, May 17, 2026, to Sunday, July 26, 2026. The dates are subject to change.
Check-in will occur on Sundays from 1:00 p.m. to 5:00 p.m. in the Pacer Commons office.
You may vacate your assigned space earlier than the date on your application, but no refund will be processed.
If you need an early arrival or late departure, please email Datasia Padgett at datasia.padgett@usca.edu. Please note that this will only be accepted on the availability of residential rooms and is not guaranteed. Any rental period not falling between the dates above must secure approval from the Director of Housing and Residence Life.
Information on Residential Building
All Summer Interns will be housed in Pacer Commons during the summer 2026 period. Pacer Commons is a furnished residence hall building with a full kitchen, living room area, two bedrooms (Double style)/ four bedrooms (Single style), two bathrooms, and closets. University Housing will be making all housing assignments before arrival on campus. Click here to view more about Pacer Commons.

Intern organizations paying for their interns housing cost must complete the Intern Invoice Form below. If the sponsoring organization does not complete the form, all payments will be returned to the intern. Only one form will need to be completed if an organization is sponsoring more than one intern, but each intern will need to complete the Summer Intern Housing Application individually.
Insurance
University Housing strongly encourages incoming interns to acquire their renter’s insurance before moving onto campus. USCA nor University Housing is responsible for damages to or losses of your personal property as stated in the University Housing Community Guide.
