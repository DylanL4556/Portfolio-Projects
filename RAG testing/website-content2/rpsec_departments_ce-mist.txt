Center of Excellence in Middle-level Interdisciplinary Strategies for Teaching (CE-MIST)
Dr. Gary J. Senn, Director
Dr. Deborah McMurtrie, Program Director
Dr. Bridget Coleman, Curriculum Coordinator
CE-MIST aims to develop and model state-of-the-art in-service and pre-service teacher training programs that concentrate on interdisciplinary curriculum development and implementation at the middle school level. CE-MIST will develop innovative practices that enable school personnel to improve student achievement, provide effective, sustained, high-quality professional development, incorporate field-based teacher education programs that involve technology-based instructional techniques, and implement innovative practices for teaching children with diverse backgrounds and diverse learning styles. CE-MIST will model these programs as it provides professional development for higher education faculty around the state of South Carolina.
CE-MIST is made possible under a grant from the South Carolina Commission on Higher Education under the auspices of the Education Improvement Act (EIA) Teacher Education Centers of Excellence Grant Program.
Goals and Objectives
Goal 1: Developing and modeling exemplary teacher training programs.
Goal 2: Providing hands-on, inquiry-based, research-supported programs.
Goal 3: Developing an influential constituency for the Center
Goal 4: Achieving a position of leadership in the state
Goal 5: Develop a detailed research agenda
