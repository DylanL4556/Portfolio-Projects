Advanced Manufacturing Collaborative (AMC)
SRNL Advanced Manufacturing Collaborative, the national lab’s advanced manufacturing facility and the home of the USCA Engineering Department.
The Savanah River National Laboratory’s Advanced Manufacturing Collaborative (AMC) will be an innovation hub for manufacturing, fostering modern industrial practices, advancing new technologies, and training the future manufacturing workforce with a focus on chemical and materials manufacturing.
The AMC is a true public-private partnership, combining the unique capabilities of the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) National Laboratories, industrial enterprises, and educational institutions to drive the long-term sustainability of the U.S. manufacturing sector. This facility will be one of only seven national laboratory facilities located on a university campus.
The 60,000-square-foot facility will not only provide new laboratory, office, and conference space suitable for advanced manufacturing research and development, but more importantly, it will house collaborative and research and development spaces for launching innovative technologies into the commercial sector.
The USCA Engineering will have several offices, laboratories, and classrooms in the new AMC building. The academic and research spaces will provide significant collaborative opportunities between USCA faculty/students and SRNL/AMC staff.
