3D printing and prototyping
Purpose
The collaborative initiative between the departments of Engineering and Chemistry enhances the array of design, 3-D printing, and prototyping capabilities available to USCA and its regional partners. This joint effort is poised to offer a comprehensive suite of resources, fostering innovation and supporting a wide range of projects.
Capabilities
At USCA, we have a diverse array of 3D printers, each offering unique capabilities to meet various needs. The Bambu Lab P1S stands out for its advanced multi-material printing capabilities and high-speed performance, making it ideal for professional and hobbyist applications. The Snapmaker J1S, known for its modular design, offers 3D printing, laser engraving, and CNC carving, making it a versatile tool for creators. Raise3D E2 features independent dual extruders (IDEX), enabling efficient dual-material or dual-color prints and mirroring mode for increased productivity. The Intamsys Funmat HT excels in industrial-grade 3D printing with high-temperature capabilities, suitable for engineering-grade materials like PEEK and ULTEM. The Snapmaker A250 integrates 3D printing, laser engraving, and CNC carving in a larger build volume, catering to various creative projects. Elegoo Saturn S offers a larger build volume and high-resolution printing using MSLA technology, providing detailed and high-quality prints that are perfect for enthusiasts and professionals. Lastly, the Elegoo Mars 4, with its monochrome LCD and upgraded resolution, delivers fast and precise prints, making it an excellent choice for detailed models and prototypes.
