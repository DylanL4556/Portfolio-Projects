Emotional Support Animals
University of South Carolina Aiken recognizes the tremendous benefit an Emotional Support Animal (ESA) can provide. Per federal and state law, ESAs may be permitted to reside in the residence halls when approved as a housing accommodation. Students must follow the same request process as any other housing accommodation. The student must complete the student application for registration and submit verification of a disability with documentation to support an ESA. Upon receipt of the required documentation, the Coordinator of Disability Services will review and decide as to whether there is sufficient documentation to support a request to have an ESA.
Please be aware that much misinformation is available about what constitutes legitimate medical or otherwise supportive documentation for an ESA. Many licensed therapists nationwide are willing to write letters supporting an ESA based on a single conversation in person, over the phone, or on a tele-meeting platform (i.e., zoom). These brief conversations often do not establish an appropriate treatment relationship between a student and the provider, so the documentation may not be applicable. Additionally, to date, the university is not aware of any official national or state accreditation or certification for ESAs, despite there being many online organizations claiming as such. Please contact the Office of Disability Services to discuss whether or not a certain source of documentation would be deemed legitimate and valid.
Documentation Guidelines
For documentation to be considered, it must come from an appropriately licensed professional with whom the student has an established treatment relationship related to the requested accommodation. The treatment provider must submit the information on their professional letterhead, typed or written and legible, and must date and sign the document.
To determine if a particular housing accommodation is necessary, the documentation provided should include the following:
- Description of disability or impairment.
- Information about the current functional impact of the condition or disability as it relates to the housing modification or accommodation requested.
- Recommendation from the professional explaining the need for the accommodation as it relates to a disability/impairment.
- If requesting an Emotional Support Animal (EAS), information about how the ESA is part of a student’s ongoing treatment and necessary to reduce the impact of a specific set of symptoms or functional impairment should be included.
