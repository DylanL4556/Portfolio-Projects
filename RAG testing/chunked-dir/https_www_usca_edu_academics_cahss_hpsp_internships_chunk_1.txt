Internships
USCA student on the job at his internship with the Savannah River Site Museum
An internship can round out your history or political science degree perfectly, giving you an opportunity to try out your chosen career, useful work skills, and an advantage when you apply graduate school, law school, or your first job.
Recently, our students have interned at sites including:
- Aiken County Solicitor’s Office
- Gregg Graniteville Archive
- Matson Consulting
- Redcliffe Plantation
- Representative Joe Wilson’s Office
- Savannah River Site Museum
- USCA Advancement Office
History Majors
If you are a history major and are interested in an internship, reach out to Dr. Sarah King, history internship coordinator.
Political Science Majors
If you are a political science major and are interested in an internship, reach out to Dr. Sarah Young, political science internship coordinator.
The history and political science programs are pleased to offer financial support, in the form of stipends or expense reimbursements, to our students who obtain internships that prepare them for careers in history, political science, and related fields. Once you have secured your internship and a faculty advisor, click here to apply for funding.
