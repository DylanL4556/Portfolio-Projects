Binge Eating/Compulsive Overeating Disorder
Like bulimia, this disorder entails consuming a large amount of food in short periods with an accompanying sense of being out of control. However, in this case, the food binge is not followed by a purging episode. Often, people with this disorder eat very quickly, feeling as if they cannot stop. Or, eat until they are uncomfortably full, after which they feel intense shame. Binge eaters/compulsive overeaters often exhibit symptoms of depression such as isolation and mood swings.
Symptoms may include:
- Yo-Yo dieting
- preoccupation with food
- fear of not having enough to eat
- fear of not being full
- feeling of guilt
WHAT ARE THE WARNING SIGNS?
The more warning signs a person has, the higher the probability the person has or is developing an eating disorder.
