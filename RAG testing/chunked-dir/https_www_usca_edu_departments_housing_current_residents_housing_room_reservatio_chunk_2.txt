Rules and Guidelines for All Spaces in University Housing
- An event must be organized by a University Housing resident, a university department or office, and/or a university-recognized student organization.
- All events must abide by the policies in the University Housing Community Guide, the University Housing Agreement, and the University Student Handbook. Failure to follow these policies will result in the event's ending by a university or University Housing staff member and the loss of the use.
- All venues are alcohol-free areas, per University Housing policies.
- All Housing facilities are booked on a first-come, first-served basis.
- Any professional staff member from University Housing can specify restrictions concerning how a space is used, depending upon the requested event type. These specifications will be provided in writing to the event coordinator, organization president, and organization on-campus advisor.
- Event organizers are responsible for the conduct of their members or guests and may be held accountable for any policy violations through the University conduct process.
- Events may not be announced or advertised to residents or students until all parties have fully agreed upon contracts and approval has been issued by a member of the University Housing staff. Advertising the event before its approval will result in University Housing denying the request for an event.
- All groups using Housing facilities must leave facilities as they found them, i.e., no trash left in the room, no changes in the room setups, no covers on doors or windows, no damage to furnishings or venue, etc. If this is not done, then damages will be charged to the student(s) who reserved the venue.
- Indoor and outdoor space is subject to the campus-wide ban on tobacco and smoking policy.
- Candles, inflammables, or open flames are prohibited from use.
- Use of tape or other adhesives, nailing, tacking, or permanently adhering items to ceilings, walls, floors, windows, or doors is prohibited in all venues.
- Fire safety equipment should not be tampered with in any space.
