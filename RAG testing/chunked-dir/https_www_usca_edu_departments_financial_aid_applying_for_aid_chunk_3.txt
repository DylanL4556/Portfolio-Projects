Summer Financial Aid
For USCA's summer sessions, you'll need to fill out the current year's FAFSA and a separate USCA summer financial aid application (for loans only), available on Self Service Aiken starting April 1. Due to the condensed time frame, it's crucial to adhere to application deadlines, with the priority deadline set for May 15.
Financial aid for summer school is limited, so it's essential to plan accordingly.
- To be eligible for aid, undergraduates must be enrolled at least half time (or six hours) over all summer sessions. For graduates, half time is three hours.
If you've used all your Federal Direct Loan eligibility for the academic year during the fall and spring, there will be no Direct Loan funds left for summer sessions. However, if you're eligible for Federal Pell Grant or private educational loans, you can explore those as options for summer enrollment. Parents of dependent students may also be eligible for Federal Parent Loan (PLUS) for summer sessions.
Remember to submit your application by the deadline to ensure timely processing.
Summer Scholarships
Summer semester scholarships offer eligible students an opportunity to fast-track their degree completion at a lower cost. To qualify, students must meet specific GPA and credit hour requirements by the end of the spring term. This information applies to Palmetto Fellows and LIFE Scholarship programs, students must maintain enrollment in at least 12 credit hours over the summer term to remain eligible. Additionally, students may be eligible for enhancement funds to their scholarships under certain conditions. It's essential to meet with a financial aid counselor before disbursement. If your summer enrollment involves concurrent or transient study, you must complete a Special Enrollment Form with the Registrar's Office.
For more information on general and eligibility requirements, contact the Office of Financial Aid at (803) 641-3476 or stuaid@usca.edu.
