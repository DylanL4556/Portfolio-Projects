Want to increase your chances of getting into USCA? Enhance your academic foundation by engaging in a comprehensive college preparatory curriculum during your high school years. What courses you pick, how you score on tests, and an Admissions Index* are key to being admitted. We’re looking for students ready to lead, think big, bring their unique backgrounds to the table, and make a difference in the world.
Why Choose USCA for Your Undergraduate Studies?

- Personalized Learning Experience: Enjoy small class sizes that ensure personalized attention, fostering a deeper understanding and a more engaging learning environment.
- Career Readiness: Take advantage of comprehensive career services, internships, and co-op programs that provide practical experience and networking opportunities to prepare you for success after graduation.
- Affordable Education: Access a high-quality education at an affordable cost, with various financial aid options, scholarships, and grants available to support your journey.
- Vibrant Campus Life: Immerse yourself in a dynamic campus culture with countless clubs, organizations, and activities, ensuring a well-rounded and enriching college experience.
Requirements
Here’s what you need to be admitted as a Freshman undergraduate at USCA:
- Complete the 21 high school course prerequisites at the college prep or higher level (honors, advanced placement, dual credit, etc.)
- This includes 4 units of English, 4 units of Mathematics (Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, or Higher Level), 4 units of academic electives, 3 units of Laboratory Science (Biology, Chemistry, or Higher Level), 3 units of Social Science, 2 units of Foreign Language (must be sequential), and 1 unit of Physical Education or ROTC.
- Demonstrate strong high school performance (through curriculum attempted, grades earned, and class rank)
- Exceed minimum SAT or ACT standards (excluding writing test results)
- Score appropriately on the Admissions Index
*The Admissions Index is calculated using your high school grade point average of core college prep courses and your SAT/ACT scores. Of these two variables, your high school grade point average is the most important and has twice as much weight in the formula.
Note: The University may offer provisional admission for high school students who do not meet the requirements for regular freshman admissions but demonstrate academic potential.
