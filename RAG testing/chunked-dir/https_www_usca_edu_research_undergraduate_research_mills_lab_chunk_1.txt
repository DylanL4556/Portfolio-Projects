The Mills Lab – Fly lab
Egg Development in Understudied Insect Vectors
Research in this lab focuses on characterizing egg development in two understudied livestock pests: Stomoxys calcitrans (stable flies) and Culicoides sonorensis (midges). These blood-feeding insects serve as vectors for pathogens that affect livestock, leading to infestations and significant economic losses in associated industries. Since blood-feeding is essential for egg production, understanding the timing and molecular mechanisms behind egg development is crucial. This knowledge can help identify potential targets for innovative control strategies
Current Research Topics
1. Determining temporal gene expression dynamics across egg development
- Molecular biology
2. Characterizing non-coding RNA populations within stable flies and midges
- Next generation sequencing
- Computational analyses
3. Determining non-coding and coding RNA gene expression dynamics across egg development
- Next generation sequencing
- Computational analyses
- Molecular Biology
4. Monitoring follicular development
- Histology
