University of South Carolina Aiken, in partnership with Clemson University, is excited to bring the Call Me MISTERTM Program to our School of Education. Consequently, we hope to fulfill the mission Call Me MISTERTM by:
- Increasing the number of SC teachers from diverse backgrounds and experiences. Call Me MISTERTM (Mentors Instructing Students Toward Effective Role Models) aims to select and support future teachers from socioeconomically and culturally diverse communities.
- Recruiting, retaining, and nurturing participants. Call Me MISTERTM is a cohort model emphasizing networking, leadership, camaraderie, and achievement under the guidance and support of adult advocates.
- Building the talent pipeline across high-need areas. Call Me MISTERTM recruits outstanding future educators for teaching certification in early childhood, elementary, middle level, and K-12 certification areas.
Eligibility
To apply to the Call Me MISTERTM program at USCA, a teacher candidate must enroll as an education major in one of the following programs: Early Childhood Education, Elementary Education, Middle-Level Education, Special Education, or Music Education.
Please visit our Programs page for information on the education majors offered at USCA.
Teacher candidate participants in the Call Me MISTERTM program are largely selected from under-served, socioeconomically and culturally diverse communities.
Benefits
- Academic Coaching
- Leadership Training
- Mentor Teachers
- Scholarship Awards
- Professional Development
