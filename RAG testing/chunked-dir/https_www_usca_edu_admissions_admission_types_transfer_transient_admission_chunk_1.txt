Students from other colleges or universities (outside the USC system) wishing to enroll at USCA to take courses they plan to transfer back to their home institution are considered transient students.
Transient students are responsible for determining if the courses they plan to take at USCA will transfer to their current college or university. Transient students are not eligible to receive financial aid through USCA but should contact their home institutions' Office of Financial Aid regarding transient financial aid eligibility.
Please note that acceptance to USCA does not guarantee enrollment into specific classes. Please refer to our Academic Bulletin for course restrictions and pre-requisite requirements. Many courses reach maximum capacity during priority registration before transient student registration.
Students from other USC campuses should contact their academic advisor or home campus registrar about the procedure they should follow to take courses on the Aiken campus.
Transient Student Admissions Process
- Meet with your academic advisor at your home institution to determine which courses would be appropriate and obtain a transient permission form signed by your home institution permitting you to take a specified course or courses at USCA. Only your college or university can approve the transferability of courses. Providing this transient permission form waives the USCA Advisement requirement.
- Complete a USCA undergraduate application (select the non-degree seeking status) and pay the $45 non-refundable application fee.
- Turn in the transient permission form from your home institution to USCA's Office of Admissions. You must provide this form to be accepted to USCA. as a transient student.
- Submit transcripts showing all college/university coursework attempted. This is required as the student may need to show proof of pre-requisite courses before registering for a specific course at USCA. Transcripts may be official or unofficial and should be sent to Admissions at admit@usca.edu.
- Verify your US citizenship or lawful immigration status - complete the Citizenship Form and return it to the Office of Admissions with supporting documentation.
- Return documentation for all required Immunizations to the Office of Admissions.
