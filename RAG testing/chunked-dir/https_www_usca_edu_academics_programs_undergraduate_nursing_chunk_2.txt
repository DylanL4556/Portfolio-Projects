Course Highlights
Note: Taking courses within the BSN program is dependent on admission to the BSN program. See Academic Bulletin and the School of Nursing website for admission requirements.
At USCA, our 121 minimum-credit-hour major in nursing starts with foundational coursework, such as nursing practice and pharmacology. From there, you'll take baccalaureate-level courses that include:
- Professional Nursing
- Health Assessment for RNs
- Pathophysiology for RNs
- Ethical-Legal Issues in Nursing for RNs
Additional courses within the nursing program include specialized areas of nursing, such as adult health, women’s health, child health, community health, and psychiatric nursing.
