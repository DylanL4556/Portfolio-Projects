USCA welcomes students from all over the world. We have students from more than 28 countries on our campus, including Canada, Angola, France, Germany, Bermuda, Bahamas, China, Brazil, Argentina, Tanzania, Nigeria and many others. International students are among our most academically and socially successful students and integral to our diverse campus. This helps enrich our campus by adding unique social, personal, and academic experiences for all our students.
Why Choose USCA for Your International Studies?
- Global Community: Join a diverse and inclusive campus that welcomes students from around the world, offering a supportive environment to learn and grow.
- Cultural Exchange: Immerse yourself in a rich cultural exchange, gaining new perspectives and sharing your own heritage within the university community.
- Comprehensive Support: Enjoy a suite of services tailored for international students, including English language programs, visa guidance, orientation, and continuous support for a smooth transition to life in the U.S.
- Career Opportunities: Take advantage of internships and job placement services that cater to the unique needs of international students, providing valuable work experience in a global context.
- Strategic Location: Experience life in Aiken, South Carolina, known for its warm climate, hospitality, and proximity to major cities, offering a blend of tranquility and access to urban opportunities.
Requirements
To apply as an international student, please follow the same application process as domestic students:
All international applicants must submit the following items:
- Application with $100 Non-Refundable submission fee
- Official school records, transcripts, or examination certificates
- Must include certified, literal (exact) English translations for documents not originally issued in English. All official documents must be sent directly from the issuing institution, examining body, evaluation service, or translation service to the USCA Admissions Office.
- Financial certification as required by U.S. immigration law. (Form)
- Laws mandate that international students demonstrate they have sufficient funds for their studies. The Admissions Office requires original financial documents, not photocopies, proving you can cover one year's non-resident expenses at USCA.
SAT or ACT examination results are optional for admission, but mandatory for potential scholarship consideration.
Additional items are needed for Graduate-seeking international students:
- Course-by-Course Evaluation
- Applicants must submit a "course-by-course" evaluation with GPA from a NACES-member credential service, sent directly to USCA Admissions. Student-provided copies are not accepted for official decisions. All evaluation fees and communication are the student's responsibility.
- Course descriptions in English are optional but helpful to use in conjunction with the Course-by-Course Evaluation.

English Proficiency
Applicants who do not use English as their language of everyday use must demonstrate proficiency in spoken and written English. This helps ensure students who speak English as a second language are able to participate fully in the classroom and be successful both academically and socially.

Conditional Admission
International students from non-English-speaking countries who meet all admissions criteria except the English requirement may apply for conditional admission to USCA without a TOEFL or IELTS score. Conditional admission indicates academic eligibility, but full admission and class enrollment require proving English proficiency through USCA’s TOEFL/IELTS standards or completing specific levels at a partner IEP.
In order to apply for conditional admission, follow the standard application process for international freshman or transfer students. Then, express your interest in conditional admission by emailing the Admissions Office.
International Cost Overview
Tuition and fees at USCA are the same for international students as they are for domestic non-resident students, but there are documentation requirements.
The cost of attendance is the total amount of tuition, fees, room and board, books, supplies, and other estimated school expenses.

Services & Programs for International Students
The Office of International Programs provides immigration education and support to USCA international students and their families. The office also provides programs to assist international students with adjusting and integrating into USCA, the local community, and American culture.
