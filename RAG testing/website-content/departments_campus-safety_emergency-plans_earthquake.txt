Remain calm and quickly follow the steps below:
- If indoors, remain indoors, drop to the ground, and cover your head. Stay away from glass windows, shelves, and heavy equipment. Attempting to rush outside may cause injury
- If outdoors, move quickly away from buildings, utility poles, power lines, and other structures. If possible, meet with BECs at your rally point for further instructions. Listen to the emergency PA system for further information.
- After the initial shock, evaluate the situation. If emergency help is necessary, call USCA Police at 6-1-1-1. Protect yourself at all times and be prepared for aftershocks.
- Coordinate with your BEC/supervisor/faculty and maintenance personnel to turn off all potentially hazardous equipment, i.e., gas/electric shut-off valves and appliances. Do not take unnecessary risks.
- Report potentially dangerous damage to facilities to the USCA Police at 6-1-1-1.
- If necessary, or if directed, the BEC will evacuate the building.
- Evacuate the building by quickly walking to the nearest exit. Be aware of structural damage and assist both the disabled and injured.
- Once outside, move to parking lot rally point away from the affected building(s). Keep away from utility poles and other structures.
- To the best of your ability, without re-entering the building, assist Police officers or BECs in their attempts to determine that everyone has evacuated safely. Do Not return to a building until told to do so or the all-clear signal is sounded (emergency signal pulse steady)
- If the Chancellor’s Office signals a campus-wide evacuation (emergency signal steady tone), evacuate the campus following the PA announcement, emergency web page, and text messaging instructions.
