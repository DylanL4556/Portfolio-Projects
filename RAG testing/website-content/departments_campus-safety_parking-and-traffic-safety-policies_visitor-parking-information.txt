Visitors to the Penland Administration building are welcome to park in the visitor spaces in the circle in front of the building, or white-lined spaces.
Visitors to the Supply & Maintenance building are welcome to park in the visitor spaces in front of the building, or in the white-lined spaces.
All yellow-lined spaces on campus are reserved for Faculty/Staff, Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. – 2 p.m.
Visitors to campus may park without a parking pass in any non-housing lot in white-lined spaces until Midnight and until 2:00 a.m. Friday and Saturday. All unidentified/ unregistered (with no USCA decal) vehicles on campus will be booted or towed Sunday through Thursday from Midnight to 6:30 a.m. and Friday and Saturday from 2:00 a.m. to 6:30 a.m. at the owner’s expense.
Visitors to University Housing may only park in Lot D, and the same rules and hours apply as above. An Overnight Guest of a Resident student must come to University Police with their host, to obtain an Overnight Guest Parking Pass. A Resident may not obtain a Guest Parking Pass for a member of the opposite sex. Overnight Guests must park in Lot D. The Guest and Resident Host must each show a valid picture ID. Additionally, the Guest must present the vehicle registration.
Motorcycles, scooters, and mopeds do not require a decal; however, they must be parked only at cycle pads. There are designated motorcycle parking spaces at each housing area for residents, by the Gregg-Graniteville Library, and the Student Activities Center.
Violators of any USCA Parking policies above are subject to fines and/or towing. If your vehicle is booted or towed, please contact University Police at 648-4011 or stop by the Station. We’re open 24/7.
If you have any concerns, please call University Police at 803-641-3319 or x3319 on-campus.
